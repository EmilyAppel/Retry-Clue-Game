You check the revolver. It's an antique your father's had since you were a child. It doesn't work anymore, but maybe that wasn't the point of the message.

You look closer and suddenly notice a rolled up piece of paper in the barrel of the gun. You take it out and unroll it. It's from your father. It reads...    

 

Dear Henry,

If you're reading this, then my suspicions have been confirmed. Someone is plotting against me.

I've heard rumors for a while. With my age and weakening strength, it's unsurprising that someone would be looking for the chance to off me and steal my fortune. But I must admit, I thought I'd have more time.

I think someone at this party plans to kill me. I heard them talking on the phone about "waiting until he's alone," but I couldn't make out who it was. I think it was a woman's voice? 

Regardless, I'm currently trapped in a house with a potential murderer hiding amongst a group of friends. I don't know who I can trust. And the minute that they start to leave, the closer the numbers will dwindle until I'm alone with the killer. Just as they planned.

I don't have much time. I hear footsteps and I need to find a safe place to hide until I can come up with a way to defend myself.

I'm taking th ehidden passage to my study.  That should buy me some time. If you don't remember where that is, check the book in the hallway. It'll guide you there.

I'll try to hold out as long as I can. Please bring reinforcements as soon as possible. Or if it truly is too late for me, I will try to leave as many clues as I can in my stead so that you can bring whoever is responsible to justice.

 

 Until we meet again,

George
